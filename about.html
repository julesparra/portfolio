⚡ Nice to meet ya!

I'm Jules (she/they), an information systems and Spanish student at the University of Maryland. I'm a proud queer, latinx person.


💡 What I'm up to

  • As a Technology Organizer at Technica, the world's largest all-female and non-binary hackathon, I develop gotechnica.org, which hackers use to learn about and register for the event. As Technica 2020 went all-virtual and international for the first time given COVID-19, I also conducted user testing sessions of the custom virtual platform to propose and prioritize UI/UX changes. incorporating more accessible design and development practices and working with talented designers and developers. Check out our projects on GitHub.

  • As a member of the QUEST Honors Program — the highly selective joint program with the Robert H. Smith School of Business; A. James Clark School of Engineering; and College of Computer, Mathematical, and Natural Sciences — I collaborate with business, engineering, and science students to consult for UMD offices, local businesses, and global companies.

Through collaborating in and leading diverse, multidisciplinary teams at the intersection of business, technology, and design, I strive to work on modern, international, and accessible technology solutions.

Professor Dr. Logan Williams (Fall 2019)

Explored and assessed the impact of engineering technology on society and the role of society in generating that technology through regular discussion, case studies, independent research on a current ethical case, a team-based hands-on ethics lesson, and a personal ethics portfolio. Employed and practiced critical thinking, collaboration, and presentation skills.

Advanced Grammar and Composition I (SPAN301)

Profesor Jackson Monzón (Fall 2019)

Alongside grammar and culture lessons with regular discussion, composed several journalistic pieces including a persuasive essay, narration of a historical event, and an editorial, as well as a cover letter. Technology, Education, and Society: Ecuador in Context (CPSP279T)

Professor Timothy Reedy (Winter 2020)

Study abroad practicum course for Science, Technology, and Society Scholars Program. Traveled through Guayaquil, Saraguro, Cuenca, and coast (Puerto López, Isla de la Plata, and Montañita) of Ecuador. Coursework included regular Spanish classes, lectures and site visits on local technological projects, planning and teaching science lessons in Spanish to elementary students in the indigenous Shiña community in Rañas, writing an independent research paper through in-country interviews and outside investigation — mine is on the economic, political, and social effects of the dollarized economy —, and a group reflection video. Practiced Spanish through interactions with university students, host family, and local people.


Approaches to Cultural Materials in the Hispanic World (SPAN303)

Profesora Carmen Benito-Vessels (Spring 2020)

Read and analyzed short stories, essays, and poems throughout history. Completed a research paper and presentation on a Spanish work of my choosing: "Cómo refleja la naturaleza los problemas de la humanidad: Los papeles del escenario en El hijo por Horacio Quiroga" (Translated: "How nature reflects the problems of humanity: the roles of the setting in "El hijo" by Horacio Quiroga.") Entirely in Spanish.

Developed and proposed recommendations to improve the virtual onboarding and orientation process; final deliverables consisted of a client report and poster presentation.

Advanced Communication I (SPAN311)

Alongside grammar and culture lessons with regular discussion, composed weekly essays about various modern issues, ranging from social movements in Latin America to the repercussions of being politically correct. Professor Dr. Nicole Mogul (Fall 2020)

Capstone course for Science, Technology, and Society Scholars Program. Developed and presented a lesson on science communication to local high school science students to introduce them to the concept and its role in our world through hands-on activities and discussion.

Participated in hands-on weekly classes and lab assignments on various core Amazon Web Services (AWS) products: Identity Access Management (IAM), Simple Storage Service (S3), Elastic Compute Cloud (EC2), Databases, Route53, Virtual Private Clouds (VPCs), High Availability architecture, Applications, and Serverless. Attended a guest lecture with a Data Engineer at Accenture on how he uses containers (ECS) and Kubernetes (EKS) and participated in a workshop on containers using the AWS Command Line Interface (CLI)/Git and Docker. Completed case studies on how Sweetgreen, Robinhood, and Netflix use AWS and what other services they could implement.
